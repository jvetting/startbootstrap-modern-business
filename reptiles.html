Black Snakes

Growing up to 80 inches long (6.5 feet), Black Snakes are very helpful in eliminating rats and mice. Despite being harmless to humans, Black Snakes are intimidating to look at and may need to be removed.

Treatment

Our skilled team will locate the snake and diagnose the most effective trap for the situation.

Copperhead

The Northern Copperhead is the only venomous snake in the DC metropolitan area, with a deep reddish brown color with dark hourglass-shaped markings on its back, this predator can grow to 3 feet and prefers rocky, wooded areas.

Treatment

Our experienced exterminators will follow the clues to locate the snake in question and employ an efficient trap.

Garter Snakes

The Eastern Garter Snake is a common snake known for the three white or yellow stripes that run down its back. Garter snakes are non-venomous and eat earthworms, millipedes, spiders, insects, salamanders, small fish, frogs, and toads. Despite being non-venomous, garter snakes still pose a threat in their aggressive territorial nature, biting humans and sometimes causing allergic reactions.

Treatment

Our experts will follow the clues left by garter snakes to leave baited traps in areas that will bring the best results.

Water snakes

The northern water snake is an aquatic snake that dwells in lakes, swamps, streams, and other waterways around the Chesapeake Bay watershed. With a heavy body, large rounded head, and a pattern of black or reddish blotches on their backs, water snakes are non-venomous and aggressive when threatened.

Treatment

Our team will follow the most minute hints to employ the most effective traps in the most efficient locations.
