Carpenter Ants

Native to the eastern seaboard, Carpenter ants make their homes inside the same wood that houses people like you and me. Carpenter Ants are drawn to wooden structures, as opposed to fallen trees, due to the insulation and heating. Once they are inside, they will create passages and chambers to live in, leading to significant structural damage. The leavings of Carpenter Ants can be distinguished from Termites because Carpenter Ants will leave large amounts of debris similar to sawdust.

Treatment

Our skilled team will diagnose the level of infestation at your home or business and choose the most efficient method of treatment from our line of sprays.

Sugar Ants

Sugar Ants in the United States are the generic grouping of many different families of ant, all sharing a preference for sweet and greasy food scraps. These ants find their indoors, through the smallest crack, tear, or gap, to eat food and ruin your space.

Treatment

Our expertise in the field of extermination will ensure that steps will be taken to formally identify the individual family of ant in question. We will use this information to find the correct treatment for the job based on the unique species and severity of infestation.

Odorous House Ants

Smelling similar to rotten coconut, Odorous House Ants stink when stepped on and will assault your nose. They are attracted to sweet foods and water and are found during the spring and fall.

Treatment

Our skilled professionals will use clues, such as leavings and sightings, to find the source of infestation and the right method of treatment. If other methods fail, our dogs can be deployed to follow the smell to the source.

Argentine Ants

Preferring sweet foods, Argentine Ants will eat almost anything and can create colonies covering an entire backyard. If left unchecked, they can contaminate food and food surfaces with their bodily waste.

Treatment

Our team will identify locate and search the colony for any and all openings before using the appropriate method for treatment. The method of treatment varies based on the severity of infestation and size of the colony.
