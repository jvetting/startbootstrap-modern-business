Most Common Infestations

Flies

From houseflies to fruit flies, they can carry many different diseases such as food poisoning bacteria.

Bed Bugs

After quickly spreading throughout a home, these parasites will feed on human blood while evading detection within the very beds we sleep upon.

Ants

Ants venture into houses in search of food to grow intricate colonies in lawns and warm, dry soil. There are many different species with different treatments necessary.

Wasps

Wasps can pest local animals in your yard and harm you with their stings. Nests can be found in wall cavities, trees, bushes, and underground and can contain over 25,000 wasps.

Rats

Rats seek warm shelters, such as homes, and spread disease through their droppings while searching for food. They chew through walls and cables, causing structural damage and fire hazards.

Termites

Among the most destructive pests in the U.S., Termites cause an average of $7,000 to $8,000 to homes, which is not insured by most house insurance policies.

Mice

Smaller than rats, Mice will similarly nest in warm shelters such as walls and closets. Constantly gnawing, they will damage furniture and food packaging while spreading disease in the process.

Cockroaches

Cockroaches live in the kitchens and walls of warm homes, coming out at night to eat any type of food and leaving diseases in their wake.

Mosquitoes

With over 150 species in the U.S., Mosquitoes come out during warm temperatures of spring and summer. They are host to many diseases due to feeding on a wide range of mammals.
